ACCOMMODATIONS

The first thing to consider with accommodations is how you plan to get around Los Angeles while you’re here and how long you’re staying. For people visiting for a few days or a week you should probably rent a car. It’s the fastest way of seeing a lot of the things that LA has to offer, it also means you can visit more exclusive beaches or hike in more rugged and pristine wildernesses. If you’re only coming for the weekend maybe it’s better to just stay at a hotel downtown and take Lyfts/Ubers around.

So, the main determiner about where to stay in Los Angeles is really what you’d like to do while you’re here, other than going to our wedding. Would you rather spend your time in LA relaxing in a nice house with a good view? With a pool? Near piles of Korean restaurants? Near the beach? In a crystal? In a car? Close to fine arts? Close to celebrities/cults?

We didn’t book rooms at any one particular location as everyone will be here for different lengths/reasons. We made a short list of some nice hotels, although Air BnB and VRBO are good choices and might help you to get a better feel for some LA neighbourhoods. Really though, it’s up to you!

We'd be happy to help you through this process! Please email us if you have questions.

THINGS TO DO IN L.A.

Hiking

There are lots of great places to hike near Los Angeles: Griffith Park, Runyon Canyon, The Baldwin Hills, The Santa Monica Mountains & the Angeles National Forest are all right in L.A. County. The high desert is also nearby, as close as the Antelope Valley or all the way out to Joshua Tree National Park a couple hours away with lots of amazing landscape to take in.

The Beach

Go to the Beach! The Pacific Ocean is right there on the west side of town. The beach has a huge range of different communities on it, from down-to-earth San Pedro, relaxed Redondo, to tony Santa Monica and painfully wealthy Malibu (pictured here) you can bathe amongst the masses, the weird (Venice) or amongst the elect.

Looking at Art

We do this a lot. There are museums, LACMA, MoCA, The Hammer, The Broad, Huntington Gardens, The Antelope Valley Museum of the American Indian and the Fowler and plenty of art galleries. If going to a wedding in one wasn't enough for you you can spend most of your time here in one as well.

Driving Around

Likely, you'll do this alot here no matter what you decide to do, but it's one of the real pleasures here. You can do a scenic drive through the hills, see weird architecture, drive out to eat stuff, drive through long flat streets with palm trees on either side like in a 90's rap video, up and down the coast highway or winding through the canyons between The Valley and Malibu. It's actually amazing. There's also great radio.
